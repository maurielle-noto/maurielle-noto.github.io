Maurielle Noto

I'm drawn to the question of how planetary bodies remember their histories. From ancient river systems on Mars to the birth of moons around distant giants, I work at the intersection of geomorphology, disk physics, and remote sensing, building the computational tools to study them.

Global Valley Widths on Mars

Valley widths across Mars have yet to be systematically mapped. This work aims to produce the first global dataset of Martian river valley widths, helping reconstruct the planet's lost hydrological history.

Modelling Exomoon Formation

The origin of moons around giant planets remains poorly understood. This work simulates the evolution of circumplanetary disks to identify the conditions that could trigger satellite formation.
